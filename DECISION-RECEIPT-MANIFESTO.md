# Decision Receipt Manifesto

AI used to create outputs.

Now AI participates in decisions.

Soon, AI will execute actions directly through tools, scripts, APIs, agents, and workflows.

That shift changes the proof problem.

A log can show what happened.

A policy can state what should happen.

A dashboard can summarize what happened.

But when an AI-assisted decision creates consequence, the harder question is:

```text
Who had authority to let this happen?
```

That is why Decision Receipts exist.

## Why receipts exist

Receipts appear wherever memory is not enough.

A payment receipt proves value moved.

A shipping receipt proves custody changed.

An access log proves entry occurred.

A Decision Receipt proves authority moved into action.

It records the chain between:

```text
request -> evidence -> authority -> approval -> execution -> consequence
```

## Why AI decisions need receipts now

AI is moving from content generation into consequential workflows:

```text
claims
credit
fraud
hiring
security
compliance
support
procurement
healthcare
legal review
software deployment
agentic tool use
```

In these workflows, the risk is not only a bad answer.

The risk is an action that nobody can later reconstruct.

When something goes wrong, the question is not just:

```text
What did the AI say?
```

The question becomes:

```text
What evidence existed?
Who approved the action?
What scope was allowed?
What actually executed?
Did the context change?
Who owns the consequence?
```

## Human-in-the-loop is not enough

A human can be present and still lack real control.

They can be:

```text
present but rushed
present but under-informed
present but unable to stop
present but rubber-stamping
present but accountable without authority
```

Human presence is not the same as human authorship.

A Decision Receipt proves whether the human had meaningful authority, evidence, and stop-power before the action became consequence.

## What a Decision Receipt proves

A Decision Receipt should make the following visible:

```text
who requested the action
who had authority
who approved it
what evidence was checked
what scope was allowed
what tool or workflow executed
what changed after approval
who owns the consequence
whether the decision still holds
```

This makes the receipt more than an audit log.

A log is event memory.

A receipt is authority memory.

## Where Decision Receipts belong

Decision Receipts belong at the boundary where AI output becomes business consequence.

Examples:

```text
AI-assisted claim approval or denial
AI-assisted credit recommendation
AI-assisted fraud escalation
AI-assisted hiring screen
AI-assisted account restriction
AI-assisted compliance review
AI agent tool call
AI-triggered deployment
AI-generated operational decision
```

Not every prompt needs a receipt.

Consequential AI-assisted action does.

## What happens without receipts

Without receipts, organizations are left with fragments:

```text
policy documents
Slack approvals
workflow logs
model outputs
screenshots
dashboards
human-in-the-loop claims
```

Those fragments may show activity.

They often fail to prove authority.

That creates the authority gap:

```text
someone is accountable for a decision they cannot prove they truly authorized, verified, or controlled
```

## The role of DAM

DAM means:

```text
Decision Authority Model
```

DAM maps the relationship between:

```text
authority
accountability
evidence
action
consequence
```

A Decision Receipt is the proof object DAM produces or governs.

DAM asks:

```text
Should this action be allowed, in this context, under this authority, with this evidence?
```

The receipt answers later:

```text
This is why the action was allowed, what happened, and whether that basis still holds.
```

## The AI Execution X-Ray

The AI Execution X-Ray is a diagnostic for one AI-assisted workflow.

It asks:

```text
Where should Decision Receipts exist but do not?
```

It looks for:

```text
authority gaps
evidence gaps
human oversight gaps
tool/action boundary gaps
scope drift
missing approval records
missing consequence owners
missing reopen triggers
```

The output is a map of where the workflow can fail reconstruction.

## Core thesis

AI is making consequence move faster.

Receipts exist when memory is not enough.

Decision Receipts are how organizations prove that AI-assisted actions were authorized, evidenced, bounded, executed as approved, and reopened when their basis changed.

## Short version

```text
If AI helped make the decision, where is the receipt?
```
